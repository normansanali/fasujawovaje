Thomas Martineau’s paralysis

Shortly after welcoming his second child into the world, Martineau encountered a life-altering incident while heading home from work.

A car recklessly disregarded a stop sign, leading to a T-bone collision with Martineau’s vehicle.

Also, this unfortunate event resulted in a devastating T3 complete spinal cord injury, rendering him paralyzed from the waist down.

The third thoracic vertebra (T3) controls the nerves to the lungs, bronchial tubes, and other chest muscles.

An injury to this vertebra can result in breathing problems like shortness of breath, wheezing, and coughing.

It may also make someone more susceptible to lung infections and illnesses like asthma, bronchitis, flu, and pneumonia.
