Top 10 NBA Players Under 6 Feet Tall

Publish date: 2024-08-06

By Fan Arch February 19, 2024 21:29

Photo by Drew Hallowell/Getty Images

In a league dominated by tall athletes, there are some exceptional players who have defied the odds and made a significant impact in the NBA despite being under 6 feet tall. These players have showcased extraordinary skills, determination, and resilience, earning the respect and admiration of fans and peers alike. Here, we explore the top 10 NBA players under 6 feet tall, highlighting their achievements and contributions to the game.

1. Allen Iverson - 6'0"

Allen Iverson, standing at 6 feet tall, had a colossal impact on the NBA during his career. Known for his exceptional scoring ability and quickness, Iverson was a prolific point guard and shooting guard. He was named the NBA Most Valuable Player (MVP) in 2001 and was an 11-time NBA All-Star. Iverson's fearless style of play and remarkable athleticism left a lasting legacy on the league.

2. Chris Paul - 6'1"

Chris Paul, at 6 feet 1 inch, is widely regarded as one of the best point guards in NBA history. Known for his court vision, leadership, and defensive prowess, Paul has consistently been among the league leaders in assists and steals. A 10-time NBA All-Star, Paul's impact on the game extends beyond statistics, as he has been a mentor to younger players and a respected voice in the NBA players' union.

3. Nate Robinson - 5'9"

Standing at only 5 feet 9 inches, Nate Robinson was an electrifying player known for his incredible leaping ability and tenacious defense. Despite his height, Robinson's athleticism and fearlessness allowed him to compete at the highest level. Robinson won the NBA Slam Dunk Contest three times and provided valuable contributions to several teams throughout his career.

4. Spud Webb - 5'7"

Spud Webb defied expectations as a 5-foot-7 point guard, captivating fans with his remarkable leaping ability. Webb's victory in the 1986 NBA Slam Dunk Contest, where he defeated much taller opponents, remains one of the most iconic moments in NBA history. His impact on the game transcended his height, inspiring shorter players and proving that size is not the sole determinant of success in basketball.

5. Muggsy Bogues - 5'3"

At a diminutive 5 feet 3 inches, Muggsy Bogues is the shortest player to have ever played in the NBA. Despite his height, Bogues displayed exceptional quickness, passing ability, and defensive skills. His 14-year career in the NBA is a testament to his determination and skill, inspiring countless individuals around the world to pursue their basketball dreams regardless of their physical stature.

6. Damon Stoudamire - 5'10"

Damon Stoudamire made a significant impact in the NBA as a 5-foot-10 point guard known for his scoring ability and leadership on the court. Stoudamire earned the NBA Rookie of the Year award in 1996 and went on to have a successful career, demonstrating that height is not a barrier to achieving success in professional basketball.

7. Earl Boykins - 5'5"

Earl Boykins stood at 5 feet 5 inches but possessed exceptional speed and ball-handling skills. Despite being undersized, Boykins had a lengthy NBA career, showcasing his ability to score and facilitate for his teammates. His resilience and determination served as an inspiration to aspiring basketball players who faced similar physical challenges.

8. Ty Lawson - 5'11"

At 5 feet 11 inches, Ty Lawson excelled as a point guard in the NBA, utilizing his speed and playmaking ability to make a significant impact on the court. Lawson's contributions to various teams throughout his career highlighted his skill set and basketball IQ, proving that height does not limit one's ability to compete at the highest level.

9. Isaiah Thomas - 5'9"

Isaiah Thomas defied expectations as a 5-foot-9 point guard, earning two NBA All-Star selections during his career. Known for his scoring prowess and tenacity, Thomas overcame doubts about his size to establish himself as a valuable asset to his teams. His journey serves as a testament to the resilience and skill of undersized players in the NBA.

10. Kemba Walker - 6'0"

Standing at 6 feet tall, Kemba Walker has been a dynamic presence in the NBA, utilizing his scoring ability and leadership to make an impact on the court. Walker, a four-time NBA All-Star, has consistently been a reliable performer for his teams, demonstrating that height is not a limitation when it comes to excelling in professional basketball.

These exceptional NBA players under 6 feet tall have shattered stereotypes and proven that skill, determination, and heart are the true measures of success in basketball. Their contributions to the game have inspired countless individuals and reaffirmed the notion that greatness knows no height limitations in the world of basketball.
