What is Claire Lius Net Worth: Biography, Net Worth &amp; more

Publish date: 2024-09-13

Claire Liu is an American professional tennis player who has amassed a net worth estimated at US$7 million as of 2023, according to CAknowledge. The 23 year-old primarily earns her million from her tennis career and lucrative brand endorsement deals.

Who is Claire Liu?

Claire Liu was born on May 25, 2000 in Thousand Oaks, United States. She is the daughter of Chinese immigrants Wen Liu and Longbin Liu. She has one sibling, a sister called Eleanor Liu.

Liu’s tennis career began when she was very young. She participated in the junior career and had really impressive stats. In 2016, she won a junior Grand Slam doubles title at Wimbledon with partner Usue Arconada.

Among her peers, she was ranked world No. 1 in junior rankings, after winning the Wimbledon girls’ singles title, and finishing runner-up at the French Open. Throughout the years, she had solid all round performances which gained her international recognition.

In 2015, Liu qualified and made her debut in the Grand Slam. A few months later, she won her first professional title at the an Orlando clay-court tournament on the ITF Women’s Circuit. At age 14, she became the youngest tennis player since Anna Kournikova in 1996 to win a professional tournament.

Liu made her US Open debut after being granted a wildcard into the tournament. In her first two matches, she defeated Verónica Cepede Royg and ninth seed Jana Čepelová, before bowing out after a loss to 26th seed, Alexandra Panova in the final round.

In 2017, Liu granted a wildcard into the qualifying for the US Open, coming off the back of winning two ITF tournaments. She beat three opponents in the qualifying round and earned a spot in the singles main draw of the Grand Slam for the first time.

At the tournament, she lost in the first round, in straight sets to Duan Yingying. Also in the doubles main draw. Liu and her partner Taylor Johnson bowed out in the first round of the US Open.

After losing out in the first round of the Grand Slam, Liu made a comeback at the 2018 Wimbledon Championships. She won all her qualifying matches, reached the second round, where she lost to eventual winner Angelique Kerber.

Liu has won one singles title and one doubles title on the WTA Challenger Tour as well as six titles in singles and one doubles title in tournaments of the ITF Women’s Circuit. She also made her maiden WTA Tour final in Rabat losing to first-time WTA tournament finalist Martina Trevisan in 2022.
