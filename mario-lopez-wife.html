Early Life and Education

Courtney Mazza was born on October 13, 1982, in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. She is of Italian descent and grew up in a Catholic family. From a young age, she was passionate about musicals. She started her modeling career when she was 3 years old and appeared in her first musical when she was 6 years old. Her acting talent was honed at the Pittsburgh High School for the Creative and Performing Arts, where she starred in three musicals and won two Gene Kelly Awards for Best Actress. She got her Equity card at 18 and attended the University of the Arts in Philadelphia. She was one of the chosen few to join Audrey Blue, a quartet of girls, and their song “Stay” was set to launch on September 11, 2011. However, the World Trade Center attacks prevented its release. She got a chance to perform a Broadway Revue at Caesar’s Palace in Atlantic City, Las Vegas after finishing her university studies. She moved to New York City after working there for three months.

Career as an Actress

Courtney Mazza has been active in the entertainment industry since 2006. She started her career as a stage performer, appearing in several Broadway shows such as A Chorus Line, Cry Baby, and The Little Mermaid. She also had a guest role in her debut film Pittsburgh.

In 2010, she starred alongside Mario Lopez in the VH1 reality television series Mario Lopez: Saved by the Baby, which documented their life as they prepared for the birth of their first child, Gia. She also executive produced the TV special Mario & Courtney’s Wedding Fiesta, which aired on TLC in 2012 and showed the couple’s wedding preparations and ceremony.

She has made guest appearances on various television shows, such as Wendy: The Wendy Williams Show, Couples Therapy, Extra, The Meredith Vieira Show, Dinner at Tiffani’s, Celebrity Family Feud, FabLife, Ellen: The Ellen DeGeneres Show, The Talk, and Home and family. She is currently the co-host of the show ON With Mario Lopez, which features celebrity interviews, entertainment news, and lifestyle segments.

Love Life and Marriage with Mario Lopez

Courtney Mazza met Mario Lopez in 2008 when they were both working on the Broadway revival of ‘A Chorus Line’. They started dating soon after and became engaged. They tied the knot on December 1, 2012, in a lavish ceremony at Punta Mita, Mexico. The wedding was attended by their family, friends, and celebrities, such as Eva Longoria, Khloe Kardashian, and Ellen K. The wedding was also televised on TLC as part of ‘Mario & Courtney’s Wedding Fiesta’.

Courtney Mazza and Mario Lopez have three children together: Gia Francesca Lopez (born on September 11, 2010), Dominic Lopez (born on September 9, 2013), and Santino Rafael Lopez (born on July 7, 2019). The couple often shares their family moments on social media and supports each other’s endeavors.

For more than 10 years, Courtney and Mario have been generous supporters of various causes. They asked their wedding guests to give to charity instead of buying them gifts. They have also joined many charitable activities and campaigns, such as Celebrity Fight Night Foundation, Broadway Barks, Feeding America, Children’s Hospital Los Angeles, Ronald McDonald House Charities and the Eva Longoria Foundation.

Mario Lopez-Courtney Mazza’s Husband

Mario Lopez was born on October 10, 1973, in San Diego, California, to Elvira and Mario Lopez Sr., who were both immigrants from Mexico. He started learning dance at the age of three and was discovered by a talent agent at the age of ten. He made his TV debut in the comedy series a.k.a. Pablo in 1984 and then joined the kids’ show Kids Incorporated as a drummer and dancer for three seasons.

In 1989, he landed his breakthrough role as A.C. Slater, a high school athlete and jock, in the popular sitcom Saved by the Bell, which ran for four seasons and spawned two spin-offs: Saved by the Bell: The College Years and Saved by the Bell: The New Class. Lopez reprised his role as Slater in both spin-offs and also in the 2020 sequel series that followed the lives of the original characters as adults.

Lopez also pursued other acting opportunities in films and television, such as Colors, Breaking the Surface: The Greg Louganis Story, Pacific Blue, The Bold and the Beautiful, Nip/Tuck, and Holiday in Handcuffs. He also appeared on Broadway in A Chorus Line.

Besides acting, Lopez is also a successful television host and personality. He has hosted several shows, such as Extra, Access Hollywood, America’s Best Dance Crew, The X Factor USA, and Candy Crush. He has also competed in the third season of Dancing with the Stars, where he finished as the runner-up with his partner Karina Smirnoff. Lopez is also an author of several books, including a fitness book and a children’s book.
