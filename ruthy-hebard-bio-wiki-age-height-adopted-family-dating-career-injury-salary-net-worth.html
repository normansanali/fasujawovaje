Ruthy Hebard College career

2016–2017 season

Ruth Hebard was a member of a highly-touted freshman class. The class included future two-time national player of the year Sabrina Ionescu, Hebard averaged a team-best 14.9 points and 8.5 rebounds per game. On January 15, 2017, Hebard recorded a season-high 29 points on 11-of-15 shooting. The game was against no. 17 UCLA.  Nonetheless, Hebard was named both All-Pac-12 Conference and Pac-12 all freshman in the same year; the 18th player in Pac-12 history to achieve the feat.

2017–2018 season

Hebard started 37 of Oregon’s 38 games. She helped the Ducks advanced to the Elite Eight in the NCAA tournament for the second year in a row. Nonetheless, Hebard averaged 17.6 points and a team-high 9.0 rebounds per game.

Hebard scored a career-high 30 points on 13-of-15 shooting and 14 rebounds in a game against Washington on February 9, 2018. She also set the NCAA men’s and women’s record for most consecutive made field goal attempts at 33 on that same month. This time round, she was named to all Pac-12.

2018–2019 season

Hebard averaged 16.1 points and 9.1 rebounds per game for the season. She helped the Ducks to their first-ever Final Four in the NCAA tournament. She shot 67% from the floor for the second-best shooting efficiency in NCAA.

Nonetheless, Hebard was perfect in two games where she went 9-of-9 in a win against Air Force and 10-of-10 in a win against Colorado. She was named to the All Pac-12 for the third year in a row.

2019–2020 season

On January 3, 2020, on a 104–46 rout of Colorado, Hebard scored 21 points and became the 12th player in Pac-12 history to surpass the 2,000 career points milestone. She joined Ionescu as the only two active Pac-12 players in the 2,000 club.
